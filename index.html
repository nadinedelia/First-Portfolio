WHY CODING?


Coming from an academic family of engineers and computer scientists, I was always quite tech-savvy, to the point where I taught myself some basic 90's style HTML, CSS, PHP and pretty much the whole Adobe Creative Suite during my early teenage years. I think I was building my first website at the age of 11 – which of course looked like a car crash – but hey, who cares, it was fun!

Coding was always fascinating to me – I love to solve puzzles and to challenge myself to find the right solution to a problem. What also intrigues me a lot is the fact that nowadays code and algorithms are everywhere. To understand code gives you a completely new level of knowledge of how the world works; an insight which I aspire to have. As a creative and sports professional, I understand the vast possibilities this field could have uses for – the ability to merge tech, art and movement based science for those within the sports industry is something that one (/I) could only dream of. I see computing and programming not as a rigid and dry subject, but indeed as something very resourceful. In the end, it’s still creation – especially web design. Creation and design that enriches our daily lives.
